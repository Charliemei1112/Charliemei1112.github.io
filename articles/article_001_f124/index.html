F1 24 Time Trial: Testing Every Constructor's Performance

Introduction

As a F1 fan, the most realistic and cost-effective hand-on experience I can acquire is by playing F1 simulators. Therefore, I have been playing F1 24, developed by Codemasters and published by EA Sports, for a couple month. As one of the most popular F1 simnulating games, F1 24 has been able to provide me with a realistic and immersive experience. By helping me understand the physicality of the cars on the tracks, and the dynamics of the regulations I have to follow throughout the race.

Though far enough from being a real F1 driver, the game still manages to simulate the physicality of the cars. And one of the implications is the difference in each constructor's performance. Whether you are playing Career mode or F1 world, choosing different cars will have a significant impact on your performance.

But how are these cars different from each other? To figure this out, I will be driving each car in time trial mode at Baurhin Grand Prix. The game will be recorded and the telemetry data will be analyzed from the video. Therefore we can see how each car performs on the track and how they are different from each other.

Car and Track Selection:

For the performance test, we will conduct time-trial mode with each car at qualifying pace at Baurhin Grand Prix.

Baurhin Circuit is one of most balanced track in the calender with 18 turns and 3 DRS zones, with a top speed appraoching 330 kmh

To minimize the variables, we will apply the setting following to all of the cars.

  • Increased Downforce (Preset 2) for car setup.
  • ERS is disabled, this is automatically in F1 24 time trial mode.
  • Racing Line assistance is applied
  • Steering Assist is turned off with brake assist set to low.
  • Due to the fact the game is played with controller, we will apply Anti-Locking Assist, Traction Control, and Automatic Gear Shifting.
I will drive each car with 3-5 laps to ensure that I am familiar with each car's characteristics so that I exploit full potential from each car.

Limitations

While the time-trial analysis provides some valuable insights into F1 24's constructor performance, there are still limitations that need to be recognized:

  • Human factors: I am a human driver with imperfection, so I cannot guarantee the 100% potential of the car is exploited given my driving habits and inconsistency
  • AntiLock Braking System (ABS) and Traction Control: Given the fact that the game was played with controller, it is not possible to simulate ABS and traction control because it is extremely hard to control the depth pressed on pedal and brake. Therefore, it is also not possible to analyze the throttle and brake of the car.
  • Battery Charing System: The ERS is disabled so that it is not possible to manually control the ERS deploy maximize the performance.
  • Weather conditions: The game was played in dry conditions only, not accounting for wet weather performance differences
  • Tyre Wear: The time-trail is set up with soft tyre under perfect condition, we couldn't tell how the tyre wear affects the performance of the car, especially in long distance
